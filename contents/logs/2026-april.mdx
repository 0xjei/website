title: "Mental Posture"
---

April was about clearing space. Not in the sense of tidying up, but in the literal sense of making room again. For years, I felt I had been carrying weight that wasn't mine to carry, but this month, something clicked. The relief is not just physical; it is about headspace I had forgotten existed. Conversations I had been avoiding finally happened, without drama, just honest exchanges that moved things forward. I learned to listen to the body knowing its limits, a signal that teaches the mind to stop pretending. I started being more productive without modifying any particular thing to my routines. The quality of my time has arisen. But, why?

I made _decisions_. Some were quiet, others not. All of them involved letting go of the abstract anxieties that had become familiar companions, of fears that no longer served any purpose, of chapters that had already ended even if I hadn't acknowledged it. There is a particular kind of courage in closing doors without looking back. Not because you are certain, but because you trust yourself to handle whatever comes next.

I am not claiming victory. This is not a triumph narrative. It is simply a recognition. There is still work to do, but the posture is different now. Like standing upright after months of hunching under invisible weight; the shoulders drop, the breath finds its rhythm, the spine remembers what alignment means. It is a matter of attitude, of how I stand in relation to what comes. I can move forward without dragging closed chapters with me, only the muscle memory of how to hold myself when the load returns.
