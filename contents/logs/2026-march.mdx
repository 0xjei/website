title: "Confusione"
---

March was all about _confusion_. A jumble of things thrown together, a messy heap that refuses to untangle itself. It touched everything unevenly: work, projects, the inner monologue running in the background. Some weeks felt clear. Others felt like having too many tabs open at once, losing the only one that actually mattered.

I've been here before. What I've slowly learned, through years of these ups and downs, is that the answer isn't in seeking perfection or a quick fix. I don't look for answers in systems or methodologies — systems fossilize quickly, methodologies are just processes you struggle to maintain. What I did was simply look for a kind of posture. A point to return to when the confusion becomes deafening. A way to ask myself: what is the weight I should be carrying right now? And then accept to take it.

In the midst of the confusion, I can be disoriented and still keep moving. Given the times we're all living through, that's enough.
