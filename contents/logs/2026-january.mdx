title: "Thinking vs. Executing"
---

The central tension this month is one I have carried for years: being a better thinker than executor or, more recently, the opposite. For most of the past few years, I have been executing without thinking enough. Before that, I was thinking without doing much. I'm somewhere between the two now, and this awareness has done more for me than any productivity system I've tried.

I'm writing a lot and keeping organized notes in my Obsidian vault. I've reached 100 documents, and I'm starting to see patterns because I've been thinking in writing, which is helping me tidy up my headspace.

I also started early conversations about a physical hackerspace in Sardinia, where I live. There's something about building community offline that feels more urgent than I can fully articulate, and the idea of giving back to my hometown (one of the poorest regions in Italy) is a strong motivation for me. There is hope, even in the darkest times.
